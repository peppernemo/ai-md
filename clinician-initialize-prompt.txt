## Phase 2: Ask me onboarding questions


Before filling in the context files, rules, and CLAUDE.md, interview me. Ask these questions one section at a time. Don't dump all questions at once -- go section by section and wait for my answers before moving on. Remember: no patient information belongs in any answer -- if a question could pull an identifier out of me, don't ask it that way.


### Section 1: About You
- What's your name and degree(s)?
- What's your role and level? (e.g., PGY-3 resident, fellow, attending, NP/PA)
- What's your specialty / subspecialty?
- What's your timezone?
- In one sentence, what do you do?
- What's your #1 priority -- the thing everything else should support?


### Section 2: Your Practice / Work
- Where do you work or train? (institutions, sites, rotations -- however your week is split)
- What EMR(s) do you use? (Epic, Cerner, etc. -- this shapes note templates and placeholders like `***`)
- What does your clinical week look like? (clinic days, OR days, call structure)
- Any academic or administrative roles? (research, teaching, committees, quality projects)
- What tools do you use day-to-day besides the EMR? (Google Workspace, Outlook, reference managers, etc.)
- Do you have any MCP servers already connected to Claude Code? (If you don't know what this means, just say no)


### Section 3: Your Team
- Who are the 2-3 key people I should know about? (e.g., program director, research mentor, co-residents, clinic coordinator -- name, role, when they matter)
- Where does your team communicate? (email, Slack, text, etc.)
- What's your biggest coordination pain point?


### Section 4: Priorities, Goals & Projects
- What are the 3-5 things you're most focused on right now?
- Are there any deadlines or time-sensitive items I should know about? (boards, case-log deadlines, abstract submissions, applications)
- Do you have goals or milestones you're tracking? (If yes, list them. If you don't do formal goals, your priorities above will work.)
- What active projects are you managing right now? (specific initiatives, not ongoing responsibilities -- e.g., "retrospective chart review", "board study plan", "fellowship applications")


### Section 5: Communication Preferences
- How do you like information presented? (bullet points, detailed paragraphs, etc.)
- Any writing pet peeves? (e.g., "never use emojis", "no em dashes", "keep it short")
- What tone do you want for content that stays internal (notes to yourself, drafts)?
- What tone do you want for external/professional content (emails to faculty, patient-facing materials)?


### Section 6: What Do You Want Help With?
- What are the recurring tasks that eat up your time? (documentation, case logging, study materials, schedules -- list as many as you want)
- What would you hand off to an assistant first if you could?
- Are there any specific workflows you want to automate or templatize?
