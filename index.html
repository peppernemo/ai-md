0The rule that makes this safe

No protected health information ever enters the LLM.

  • Not the name. Not the MRN. Not the DOB. Not the patient's face.
  • De-identify before you paste — age and sex only; strip the demographics header off the note.
  • Notes come out PHI-free, with *** placeholders you fill inside Epic.
  • If a source document still has identifiers, use your institution's sanctioned, BAA-covered tool (e.g. UltraViolet) — not a personal agent.

Everything below is designed around this constraint. You are responsible for what you paste — the tool can't un-see an identifier.

## Phase 2: Ask me onboarding questions


Before filling in the context files, rules, and CLAUDE.md, interview me. Ask these questions one section at a time. Don't dump all questions at once -- go section by section and wait for my answers before moving on. Remember: no patient information belongs in any answer -- if a question could pull an identifier out of me, don't ask it that way.


### Section 1: About You
- What's your name and degree(s)?
- What's your role and level? (e.g., PGY-3 resident, fellow, attending, NP/PA)
- What's your specialty / subspecialty?
- What's your timezone?
- In one sentence, what do you do?
- What's your #1 priority -- the thing everything else should support?


### Section 2: Your Practice / Work
- Where do you work or train? (institutions, sites, rotations -- however your week is split)
- What EMR(s) do you use? (Epic, Cerner, etc. -- this shapes note templates and placeholders like `***`)
- What does your clinical week look like? (clinic days, OR days, call structure)
- Any academic or administrative roles? (research, teaching, committees, quality projects)
- What tools do you use day-to-day besides the EMR? (Google Workspace, Outlook, reference managers, etc.)
- Do you have any MCP servers already connected to Claude Code? (If you don't know what this means, just say no)


### Section 3: Your Team
- Who are the 2-3 key people I should know about? (e.g., program director, research mentor, co-residents, clinic coordinator -- name, role, when they matter)
- Where does your team communicate? (email, Slack, text, etc.)
- What's your biggest coordination pain point?


### Section 4: Priorities, Goals & Projects
- What are the 3-5 things you're most focused on right now?
- Are there any deadlines or time-sensitive items I should know about? (boards, case-log deadlines, abstract submissions, applications)
- Do you have goals or milestones you're tracking? (If yes, list them. If you don't do formal goals, your priorities above will work.)
- What active projects are you managing right now? (specific initiatives, not ongoing responsibilities -- e.g., "retrospective chart review", "board study plan", "fellowship applications")


### Section 5: Communication Preferences
- How do you like information presented? (bullet points, detailed paragraphs, etc.)
- Any writing pet peeves? (e.g., "never use emojis", "no em dashes", "keep it short")
- What tone do you want for content that stays internal (notes to yourself, drafts)?
- What tone do you want for external/professional content (emails to faculty, patient-facing materials)?


### Section 6: What Do You Want Help With?
- What are the recurring tasks that eat up your time? (documentation, case logging, study materials, schedules -- list as many as you want)
- What would you hand off to an assistant first if you could?
- Are there any specific workflows you want to automate or templatize?

6Example skills

A skill is one folder with a SKILL.md that teaches the agent a single job. It triggers on a phrase or /name. A worked, PHI-safe example:

skills/clinic-note/SKILL.md — de-identified documentation

PHI-free by default 
---
name: clinic-note
description: >
  Generate a clinic note from pasted DE-IDENTIFIED clinical data (referring
  notes, imaging, EMG/NCS) using your Epic templates. Auto-selects the template
  from the diagnosis. PHI-free by default. Trigger: "clinic note", "draft note".
---

# Clinic Note

## PHI Policy
Note output is PHI-free by default: omit the patient name/MRN/DOB header entirely
(add a `.demo` dot phrase in Epic yourself). Body uses age + sex only. Reading
identifiers in the *input* is discouraged — strip them before pasting. Opt out
explicitly with "include PHI" only if your tool is BAA-covered.

## Step 1 — Detect the note type
H&P / follow-up / post-op / procedure-specific. Pick the matching template.

## Step 2 — Map the input
Pull HPI, exam, imaging read, and assessment from the pasted (de-identified) data.
Leave anything missing as `***` (an Epic wildcard) rather than inventing it.

## Step 3 — Write to your style
Attending-level Assessment & Plan: diagnosis, differential, decision-making.
Don't regurgitate the exam — cite a finding only if it changes the plan.

## Step 4 — Output

8FAQ & troubleshooting

What does it cost? Claude Pro ($20/mo) includes Claude Code and is plenty to start. Cursor and VS Code are free. If you later hit usage limits on heavy days, Max tiers exist — don't start there.
Cursor or VS Code? Either. For this workflow they're the same thing: a file tree, an editor pane, and a terminal where Claude Code runs. I happen to use Cursor. Don't overthink it.
Is my data used for training? Not once you turn "Help improve Claude" off (claude.ai → Settings → Privacy). Do it before your first session. And keep your GitHub repo private regardless.
It asks permission for everything That's by design at first. Approve individual actions until you trust the workflow, or use "always allow" for reads. Keep confirmation ON for anything that deletes or sends.
The session got slow / confused Long conversations accumulate context. Start a fresh session per task (/clear or just open a new one) — your CLAUDE.md, rules, and memory persist; the clutter doesn't.
It got something wrong Correct it, then say "remember that…" so the correction persists, or have it write the convention into a rule file. Every correction should only happen once.
Rule, skill, or memory? Think altitude. CLAUDE.md = pointers, not protocols. .claude/rules/ = universal invariants (PHI, tone). A convention that matters to only one job belongs in that job's SKILL.md. Memory = recent corrections that haven't settled — once one proves stable, graduate it into the skill or rule and let the memory go.
Is this allowed at my institution? Know your institution's AI policy. This setup handles de-identified text only — that's what makes it defensible — and anything with identifiers belongs in your institution's sanctioned, BAA-covered tool. When in doubt, ask compliance, not forgiveness.
Do I need to know how to code? No. Everything here is plain-English Markdown files. You tell Claude what you want; it writes the files. If you can write a note template, you can build a skill.

For clinicians and researchers in any specialty. The workflow is model-agnostic; the enabling constraint — not the limitation — is no PHI in the LLM.
